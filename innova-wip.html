Gas Laws

a set of physical laws that describe the behavior of gases,
relating their pressure, volume, temperature,
and the number of moles (amount) of gas

Boyle's Law

Pressure & Volume

a connection between pressure and volume. It asserts that under constant temperature, the pressure of a specific quantity of gas is inversely proportional to its volume.

Charles' Law

Volume & Temperature

A statement that the volume occupied by a fixed amount of gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature, if the pressure remains constant.

Gay Lussac's Law

Pressure & Temperature

A statement that the pressure of a given mass of a gas varies directly with the absolute temperature of the gas, that is - if the volume is kept constant.

Combined Gas Laws

Pressure, Volume, & Temperature

A mix of Boyle's law, Charles' law and Gay-Lussac's law. The relationship of pressure, volume, and temperature: As pressure increases, temperature increases and volume decreases.
